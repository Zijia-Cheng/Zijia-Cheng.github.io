My research is centered on how topology, correlations, and symmetry shape quantum materials.
I am especially interested in systems where experiment can directly reveal unexpected electronic structure.

## Themes

### Topological phases in kagome and magnetic materials

I study materials in which topology and magnetism combine to produce unusual quasiparticles, tunable band crossings,
and strong surface or edge responses. Kagome systems and magnetic semimetals are a recurring focus because they provide
clean experimental platforms for discovering new topological phenomena.

### Spectroscopic probes of electronic structure

Much of my work uses angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy together with complementary techniques such as
tunneling microscopy and nonlinear optical probes. These tools make it possible to distinguish bulk bands from surface states,
track temperature or interaction-driven changes, and connect measurements to microscopic models.

### Correlated and interaction-driven quantum matter

I am interested in materials where electronic interactions strongly reshape topology, including Kondo lattices,
charge-ordered systems, and intertwined phases. These settings often reveal richer physics than weakly interacting topological materials.

## Current Interests

- Topological states in kagome metals and magnetic materials
- Interplay of symmetry, interactions, and topology
- ARPES-based reconstruction of complex band structure
- Bulk-surface-edge correspondence in real materials
- Quantitative analysis workflows for large experimental datasets
